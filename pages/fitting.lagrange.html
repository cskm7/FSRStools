fitting.lagrange¶

fitting.lagrange(x, x0, y0)¶

Construct the polynomial over x with the least degree that passes through all points (x0, y0) using Lagrange polynomials.

Parameters:
  • x (array) – x-values.
  • x0 (array) – List of x-coordinates of interpolation points.
  • y0 (array) – List of y-coordinates of interpolation points (same shape as x0).
Returns:

Lagrange polynomial that passes through all data points.

Note

The Lagrange form is very susceptible to Runge’s phenomenon, i.e., interpolation divergence; especially when many data points are used.

Example:

# a parabola through three points
x = np.linspace(-5, 5, 64)
y = lagrange(x, [-3, 0, 3], [1, -3, 6])
